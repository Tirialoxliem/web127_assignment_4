The Importance of Accessibility to the Visually Impaired

There is an extreme amount of people in the United States that have a vision disability. There are people of all ages from young to old, and from minor vision problems to people that are completely blind. The main image above shows someone reading braille. To the right of this content is a very good video about early intervention of very young children with visual problems. If the users who navigate your website can't read your content then it really means nothing to them, and they will not stay on your website. Some things that you need to take into consideration is the size of your font; it needs to be big enough to be read by someone without great vision. You also need to consider a font that is good on the eyes to read, like a sans-serif type. You also need to make sure that all your images have an alt attribute stating what is in the image, so their image reader can read to them what they are not able to see. You also need to have good contrast between the background color and your font color to be able to see it clear, you can't have a dark background with dark font, and you can't have a light background with a light font.
