The Importance of Accessibility to the Cognitive Impaired

Cognitive impairment might be the most prevalent out of all the impairments. I myself have a cognitive impairment called ADHD, which can make task that a neurotypical brain makes look easy, actually be very difficult for myself. There are many other cognitive impairments that people struggle with that you want to make sure that you do not forget about while building your website, like autism, dyslexia, dyscalculia, dysgraphia, and many more. The main fix for this for building your web site is done by one word, the word is easy. You want everything on your web site to be easy. You want your layout to be easy, as to where people with cognitive issues do not need to search to find things that should be in a certain area, like a navigation bar should be placed at the top of your page and not the middle of the page. Also, you want to use words that are easily understood, and not big, long words that people do not use in daily life, like if you were writing a medical page for users, you would not want to use the word cephalalgia, you would use the word headache instead. The video to the left of this text is a video I like to have people watch to learn about what I have, because there are many stereotypes out there about people with ADHD that is not true.
